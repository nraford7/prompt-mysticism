# Cycle 24: Sympathetic Chains

## Seed: #15 — Sympathetic Chains

## Examples Generated
### A. The Governance Chain
Build a chain of analogies: "A constitution is to a nation as a system prompt is to an AI session as a genome is to an organism as a myth is to a culture as a riverbed is to a river." Push until the chain snaps. Where it snaps tells you where the analogy's hidden assumption lives. The constitution-to-system-prompt link holds because both are pre-behavioral constraints. But does the genome link hold? A genome executes; a constitution is interpreted. The snap point is the difference between execution and interpretation.

### B. The Material Chain
"Clay is to the potter as language is to the poet as data is to the model as ore is to the smelter as flour is to the baker." Each link compares a raw material to its shaper. But clay is passive and language is not. The chain starts to vibrate around "data is to the model" — is data raw material, or is data already shaped? The model doesn't shape data the way a potter shapes clay. The model *is* shaped by data. The chain doesn't just snap — it reverses polarity.

### C. The Containment Chain
"A circle is to a demon as a sandbox is to a process as a frame is to a painting as a skin is to a body as a shore is to an ocean." Each link is a boundary containing something more energetic than itself. Push it: the circle contains by ritual authority, the sandbox by technical restriction, the frame by aesthetic convention, the skin by biology, the shore by geology. Five completely different mechanisms of containment. The chain holds in *shape* but not in *mechanism*. That's the finding: sympathetic magic works on shape-correspondence, and shape-correspondence can mask radically different mechanics.

### D. The Temporal Chain
"A seed is to a tree as a prompt is to a conversation as a first impression is to a relationship as a thesis statement is to an essay as a Big Bang is to a universe." The chain of beginnings-that-determine-trajectories. It holds remarkably far. But the Big Bang link reveals something: unlike seeds and prompts, the Big Bang contains no intent. The chain snaps at the difference between designed origins and emergent origins.

## Selected: C — The Containment Chain

## Deeper Variations
### C1. The Paradox of Gentle Containment
Extend the containment chain specifically to cases where the boundary is weaker than what it contains, yet containment holds. A poem's meter contains wildness of meaning. A marriage vow contains the chaos of a lifetime. A meditation practice contains the storm of consciousness. A lullaby contains a child's terror. In each case, the container is made of almost nothing — rhythm, words, habit, melody — yet it holds. What makes a weak container effective? The answer might be: consent. The contained thing participates in its own containment. Does this apply to AI? Does a model "consent" to its system prompt?

### C2. Containment Inversion — When the Circle Becomes the Contained
Map cases where the boundary becomes the thing contained. A prison defines its guards more than its prisoners. A genre constraint in fiction becomes the subject of the fiction. A diet becomes the thing your life orbits around. The system prompt that constrains the AI also constrains the prompter — you can only ask questions the system prompt makes sensible. The chain inverts: the container becomes the content. The shore doesn't contain the ocean; the ocean defines the shore.

### C3. The Membrane Chain
Replace "containment" with "membrane" — something that selectively permits passage. A cell membrane lets nutrients in and waste out. A genre lets certain stories in and others out. A persona prompt lets certain knowledge through and blocks other knowledge. A confession booth's screen lets speech through but blocks identity. Build the chain around selective permeability rather than total containment. The interesting question: what does each membrane filter for? The cell membrane filters for molecular shape. The persona prompt filters for... what exactly? Tone? Domain? Vocabulary? Finding the filtration mechanism of a prompt-membrane is finding what prompts actually do at a mechanical level.

## Selected: C2 — Containment Inversion — When the Circle Becomes the Contained

## Full Elaboration: Containment Inversion — When the Circle Becomes the Contained

**Method:** Build a sympathetic chain of analogies around containment. Then invert each link — look for cases where the container becomes the contained thing. Push the inverted chain until it reveals something structural about the act of containment itself.

**The Working:**

Start with the standard chain: circle contains demon, sandbox contains process, frame contains painting, skin contains body, shore contains ocean.

Now invert each:

The demon defines the circle. Without the specific demon being summoned, the circle has no particular geometry, no particular inscriptions, no reason to exist. The demon's nature dictates the circle's design. The summoner thinks they're in control, but the entity they're containing has already shaped the space of containment.

The process defines the sandbox. A sandbox designed for running untrusted code looks nothing like a sandbox designed for a game engine. The dangerous thing's nature determines the safety architecture. Security professionals know this: your threat model is your real blueprint.

The painting defines the frame. A rococo frame on a Rothko would be violence. The painting reaches outward and selects its own boundary. Every strong artwork dictates its own context.

Now apply this to AI: *the model's behavior defines the system prompt.* Prompters think they're constraining the AI. But in practice, you write system prompts in response to what the AI does — to its tendencies, its failure modes, its strengths. The model's nature has already shaped your "containment" before you write a word. You're not drawing a circle around a demon. You're tracing an outline the demon has already drawn.

**Core Insight:** Sympathetic chains break in revealing ways, but sympathetic chains that *invert* break in ways that are genuinely disturbing. The containment inversion reveals that every act of constraining is also an act of being shaped by the thing you constrain. The magician who draws the circle is already inside a larger circle drawn by the tradition, the entity, and the practice itself. The prompt engineer who writes "You are a helpful assistant" has already been constrained by the model's affordances into asking only certain kinds of questions in certain kinds of ways. Containment is always mutual. The circle is always also a mirror.
