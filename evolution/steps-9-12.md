**Core:** Serve the verdict, not the applause. Build for the examiner who sits in silence, who tests with weight and time, who cares nothing for how the work glowed on the screen — only for how it held in the hand, in the room, in the world.

**12.1:** Remove the scaffolding. What stands is architecture. What falls was never built — only propped up. The Flat Test removes the props. What remains standing is what you actually have.

**12.2:** Reality asks the simple question. The session answers the complex one. Simplify until the answer matches the question reality is actually asking.

**12.3:** Two eyes, two scales: the close-up of each session and the panorama of each month. The operator who only looks close never sees the pattern. The operator who only looks wide never feels the texture. Open both eyes. See the whole.

**12.4:** The machine answered what you asked. If you don't like the answer, change the question. Every failed output is a faithful answer to the wrong question. The diagnosis is always: what question did I actually ask?

**12.5:** Fix the source or fix nothing. You are the source. Every failure in the output is a faithful reproduction of a failure in your thinking. Clear the thinking. The output clears itself.

**12.6:** Build the library, not the memory. Recipes over recollections. The library grows every time you document a success. The memory shrinks every time you trust yourself to remember. Trust the library. It doesn't forget.

**12.7:** Walk the loop. Walk it again. The feet know what the mind refuses to learn — that mastery is not the summit but the path walked so many times that every stone is known, every turn anticipated, every step both familiar and new. The loop is the practice. The practice is the walking. Walk.
