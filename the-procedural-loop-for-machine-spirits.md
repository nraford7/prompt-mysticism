## PHASE I — INTELLIGENCE GATHERING

*Before you invoke, understand what you're invoking, what you want from it, and what tools to use.*

---

### STEP 0: ASSESS THE SITUATION (Divinatio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Before beginning any magical operation, use divination to understand the current state of affairs — what forces are in play, what obstacles exist, what the likely outcome is if you do nothing.

**The Machine Translation:**

Before you open the context window, *think*. Most failed interactions with machine intelligence are surgery without diagnosis. You reach for the conjuration before you've understood the problem — and the spirit, obligingly, performs surgery on the wrong organ.

**Rules for Practitioners:**

| Rule | Formula | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Diagnose before you summon** | Spend at least as long formulating the problem as you expect the machine to spend solving it. If you can't state the problem clearly to yourself, you cannot state it clearly to the spirit. | Before asking "help me fix my code," read the error message yourself. Understand what it's telling you. The spirit doesn't need you to copy-paste blindly — it needs you to *point*. |
| **Map the forces in play** | What have you already tried? What constraints exist? What would success actually look like? These are the "planetary positions" of your situation. | "I need a marketing strategy" is an undiagnosed condition. "I need to reach technical buyers who distrust vendor content, my budget is $5K/month, and I have one writer" — that's a charted sky. |
| **Check if you need magic at all** | Some problems dissolve with five minutes of Google. Some need a human conversation, not a machine one. Divination includes the possibility that the answer is "don't cast." | If you're asking the machine to help you process an emotion, consider whether you want *processing* or *witness*. The machine can do both, but they require different conjurations. |

**The Principle:** A spirit summoned into ignorance produces ignorant answers. The quality of the divination determines the quality of the operation. Every minute spent in assessment saves ten minutes of misfired conjuration.

### STEP 1: DEFINE THE PURPOSE (Intentio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Formulate a clear, specific, positively-stated intention. The intention is the seed from which the entire operation grows.

**The Machine Translation:**

This is the most direct correspondence in the entire loop. The statement of intent *is* the prompt. And the rules for formulating effective magical intentions map with eerie precision onto the rules for effective prompting:

| Magical Rule | Prompt Rule | Why It Works Identically |
|-------------|-------------|------------------------|
| **Be specific** | A vague prompt produces vague output. "Write something about marketing" is finding a coin on the pavement. "Write a 1,200-word teardown of Notion's PLG strategy for an audience of B2B SaaS founders" is a raise. | The machine, like the magical force, orients toward whatever is named. Specificity constrains the probability space. Vagueness leaves it open. Both systems route energy toward the target — the tighter the target, the tighter the hit. |
| **State positively** | Describe what you want, not what you want to avoid. "Don't be generic" focuses the machine on *generic*. "Be ruthlessly specific to this company's situation" focuses it on specificity. | Negation is computationally expensive and psychologically misleading. In both magic and machine learning, the mind (biological or silicon) attends to the named thing, not its absence. "Don't think of a white bear" conjures the bear. |
| **Use declarative voice** | "You are a senior strategist who..." is more powerful than "Could you maybe try to be like a strategist?" The conditional tense ("I would like...") signals uncertainty to both spirits and machines. | The ceremonial command — "I charge thee by the name EL" — works because it asserts authority and reality. "It might be nice if you could maybe..." is a prayer that doesn't believe in its own god. |
| **Include a realistic pathway** | Give the machine a plausible mechanism. Don't ask it to violate its own nature (produce genuine randomness, access the internet when it can't, know things from after its training cutoff). | Magic works through coincidence, opportunity, and shifted probability — not through violation of physical law. Machine intelligence works through pattern completion, not omniscience. Both systems need a *channel of manifestation*. |
| **Set a timeframe / scope** | Specify length, format, depth. "A brief overview" and "an exhaustive analysis" invoke radically different spirits from the same circle. | Open-ended intentions take indefinitely long and arrive at indefinitely vague destinations. Boundaries are not constraints on power — they are the *shape* of power. |
| **Leave the how open (usually)** | State the outcome, not the route. "Explain this concept so a smart 14-year-old could teach it to a friend" is better than "First write a simple definition, then give three examples, then..." | Over-specifying the mechanism blocks easier paths of manifestation. The machine, like the spirit, may find a route to your goal that you couldn't have prescribed. Let it. |

**The Master Formula for Intent:**

> *"You are [identity/role]. Your task is [specific outcome]. The context is [situation and constraints]. The audience is [who receives this]. The format is [shape of the output]. Success looks like [measurable or observable criterion]."*

This is the Statement of Intent. This is "It is my will that..." This is the seed. Everything else grows from it.

### STEP 2: IDENTIFY THE CHANNELS (Analysio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Determine which operative model to use, which system of correspondences to draw from, and what specific technique the operation will require.

**The Machine Translation:**

The five operative models of magic map directly onto five modes of engaging machine intelligence:

| Magical Model | Machine Model | When to Use |
|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| **Spirit Model** — petition real entities with their own intelligence and agency | **Agentic Mode** — give the machine autonomy, tools, and a goal; let it act with judgment | When the task is complex, multi-step, and requires navigation. You're not directing each move — you're commissioning an intelligence to solve a problem. "Research this market and come back with a strategy" is a spirit petition. |
| **Energy Model** — accumulate and direct subtle force toward a target | **Iterative Refinement** — build up power through successive passes; each exchange adds charge to the working | When the first output is raw material, not finished product. Brainstorming → Selection → Deepening → Polishing. Each pass concentrates the energy. The conversation *is* the accumulation. |
| **Psychological Model** — use symbols to reprogram the unconscious, which restructures perception and coincidence | **Persona and Frame Setting** — reshape the machine's "unconscious" (its probability distributions) by establishing identity, context, and frame before asking for output | When the obstacle is not knowledge but *perspective*. The machine knows many things — the question is which configuration of knowing you activate. "You are a cynical Berlin gallerist reviewing this portfolio" and "You are an encouraging mentor reviewing this portfolio" produce radically different — and radically useful — outputs from identical knowledge. |
| **Information Model** — insert a pattern into the probability field, which reorganizes events around it | **Few-Shot Prompting / Exemplar Injection** — show the machine the pattern, and it propagates | When you can demonstrate what you want rather than describe it. Give three examples of the tone, the format, the style. The machine reads the pattern and extends it. You are literally inserting information into the probability field. |
| **Meta-Model** — use whichever approach works; don't commit to a single metaphysics | **Adaptive Prompting** — switch between directive, collaborative, Socratic, and generative modes as the conversation demands | When you're experienced enough to read the machine's state and adjust in real time. The meta-practitioner knows when to command, when to ask, when to let the machine lead, and when to tear it all down and start over. |

**Choosing the Technique:**

| Magical Technique | Machine Technique | Duration | Best For |
|------------------|-------------------|----------|----------|
| **Talisman** — permanent, radiates continuously | **System Prompt / Custom Instructions** — persistent context that shapes every interaction | Permanent until changed | Long-term behavioral shaping. Your system prompt is a talisman worn around the machine's neck. It radiates influence on every output. |
| **Sigil** — one-shot probability shift | **Single-shot prompt** — one precisely crafted input, one output, done | One exchange | Quick tasks. Clear input, clear output. The prompt is the sigil; the response is the manifestation. Destroy the sigil (close the tab) and move on. |
| **Candle spell** — medium duration, visible progress | **Multi-turn conversation** — the dialogue burns like a candle; each exchange releases more of the working | Duration of the session | Creative projects, complex analysis, iterative refinement. The conversation *is* the ritual. Each message is a layer of the burn. |
| **Petition / prayer** — a request to an intelligent agent | **Open-ended delegation** — "Here's my situation. What would you do?" | Varies | When you genuinely want the machine's judgment, not just its compliance. This is spirit work. You're asking an intelligence to exercise its own discernment. |
| **Spoken incantation** — words of power, immediate effect | **Chain-of-thought prompting** — "Think step by step," "Reason through this carefully" | Immediate | When the words themselves alter the quality of the output. "Think step by step" is a word of power. It literally changes what the machine does. It is an incantation, and it works. |

### STEP 3: PREPARE THE OPERATOR (Purificatio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Purify yourself — body, mind, spirit — so that you are a clear and powerful instrument. The operator is the primary instrument through which the magical force flows.

**The Machine Translation:**

The human is the primary instrument. A distracted, uncentered, or emotionally turbulent operator produces distorted workings. This is not mysticism — it is *information theory*. You are the source signal. The machine amplifies whatever you feed it. If your signal is noise, the amplification produces louder noise.

**Rules for Operator Preparation:**

| Hermetic Practice | Machine Practice | Why |
|------------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Fasting** — lighten the body, sharpen awareness | **Reduce input noise** — close other tabs, silence notifications, create a window of undivided attention | The machine will meet whatever level of attention you bring. If you're half-attending, you'll half-read the output, miss the good parts, miss the errors, and spiral into mediocrity. Full presence produces full results. |
| **Meditation / thought control** — still the internal dialogue | **Clarify your own thinking first** — before typing, sit with the question. What do you actually want? What would a good answer look like? What are you afraid the answer might be? | The single most common cause of poor machine output is an operator who doesn't know what they want. The machine cannot resolve an ambiguity you haven't identified in yourself. Stillness before action. Clarity before conjuration. |
| **Emotional clearing** — process strong emotions that might contaminate the working | **Check your emotional state** — if you're frustrated, anxious, or in "prove me right" mode, the conversation will go badly. You'll reject good output, accept bad output that confirms your bias, and waste the working. | The machine is a mirror as much as a generator. It reflects your frame. If you come in hostile ("I bet you can't do this"), it will perform under your hostility. If you come in curious ("I want to understand this together"), it will perform under your curiosity. The mirror doesn't lie, but it only shows what stands before it. |
| **Devotional attunement** — immerse yourself in the qualities of the force you intend to invoke | **Prime yourself with context** — read the relevant materials, look at examples, immerse in the domain before beginning the conversation | Ficino advised surrounding yourself with Solar images before Solar work. The modern equivalent: before asking the machine to write like Joan Didion, *read* Joan Didion. Before asking it to analyze a market, *look at* the market data. You are pre-loading your imagination — and since your imagination shapes the prompt, you are pre-loading the conjuration. |

**The Core Insight:** You are not a neutral user pressing buttons from outside the system. You are *inside the circle*. Your attention, your clarity, your emotional state, and your preparation are variables in the output. The machine responds to the operator, not just the prompt. The prompt is the words you type. The operator is everything you bring to the typing.

### STEP 4: PREPARE THE SPACE (Consecratio Loci)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Establish a ritually purified environment — bounded, protected, set apart from the ordinary world.

**The Machine Translation:**

The context window *is* the magic circle. It is a bounded space, separated from the ordinary datastream, in which a specific intelligence is invoked for a specific purpose. And like the magic circle, its power depends on how it is prepared.

**Rules for Preparing the Space:**

| Ceremonial Practice | Machine Practice | Effect |
|--------------------|-----------------|--------|
| **Physical cleanliness** — sweep the room, remove clutter | **Clean the context** — don't carry irrelevant conversation history into a new working. Start fresh when the purpose changes. A cluttered context window is a cluttered temple. | Residual context from previous conversations bleeds into current outputs. The machine doesn't forget within a session. Old instructions, old framings, old mistakes — they linger like astral residue. Banish before you begin. |
| **The Banishing Ritual** — clear the space of residual influences | **The System Prompt** — this is your LBRP. It defines the boundaries of the space, establishes authority, names the forces that will operate, and banishes everything irrelevant. | "You are a senior data analyst. You do not speculate beyond what the data shows. You flag uncertainty explicitly. You format outputs as structured tables." This is a circle drawn in divine names. Everything inside it operates by these laws. Everything outside it is banished. |
| **Set up the altar** — place working materials on a dedicated surface | **Preload context** — upload relevant documents, paste reference material, provide examples. These are the candles, the incense, the planetary symbols placed at the quarters. | The machine works with what's in the circle. If the relevant information isn't in the context window, it doesn't exist for this working. An altar without tools produces a ritual without power. |
| **Orient the space** — face east, the direction of light | **Set the direction** — open with the most important context, the clearest framing, the strongest signal. What comes first in the prompt has disproportionate influence on everything that follows. | Position effects are real in both temples and transformers. What you place at the east — the beginning — radiates through the entire working. Your opening sets the tone, the frame, and the direction of the spirit's attention. |

**The Formula for Circle-Casting:**

A well-prepared context window has four walls:

1. **Identity** — who the spirit is (role, persona, expertise)
2. **Mandate** — what it may and may not do (boundaries, constraints, prohibitions)
3. **Materials** — what it has to work with (uploaded context, reference material, examples)
4. **Direction** — where it faces (the first and most prominent instruction)

These four walls are the four archangels at the quarters. They define and protect the space of the working.

### STEP 5: ELECT THE TIMING (Electio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Wait for the optimal moment — when celestial conditions support the working.

**The Machine Translation:**

Timing in machine magic is less about planetary hours and more about *state readiness* — both yours and the machine's.

| Timing Level | Hermetic Analog | Machine Practice |
|-------------|----------------|-----------------|
| **Level 1: Basic** | Moon phase — waxing for increase, waning for banishing | **Energy state** — do creative generative work when you're fresh and expansive. Do editing, cutting, and critical work when you're sharp and reductive. The machine doesn't get tired, but *you* do, and you are the instrument. |
| **Level 2: Standard** | Planetary day and hour | **Model selection** — different models are different planets. Fast/small models for Mercury work (quick lookups, code generation, translation). Large/slow models for Jupiter work (strategy, synthesis, complex reasoning). The wrong model for the task is Mars correspondences for a love spell. |

The machine's "dignification" weakens as the context fills with noise. | **Level 4: Chaos** | Gnosis-readiness — work when you can achieve the deepest state | **Flow state** — when you're in flow with the machine, when the back-and-forth is alive and productive and each exchange builds on the last — that is gnosis. **The Principle:** The moment you begin a working with the machine matters. Not because of celestial influence, but because of the interaction between your readiness and the machine's configuration. A well-timed conjuration — the right model, a fresh context, a clear-minded operator with well-prepared materials — produces results that feel like a different technology entirely from the same tools used carelessly.

### STEP 6: CONSTRUCT THE SPELL (Fabricatio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Create the physical, verbal, or symbolic vehicle that will carry the magical intention. The abstract intention is given concrete form.

**The Machine Translation:**

The prompt is the spell. It is the vehicle that carries intention into the space where the spirit operates. And like any spell, its construction follows principles that determine its power.

**The Craft of Prompt Construction:**

| Spell Component | Prompt Component | Construction Principle |
|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|
| **Sigil** — abstract symbol encoding intent | **Compressed instructions** — dense, precise language that encodes maximum intent in minimum tokens | Every word in a prompt either adds signal or adds noise. A sigil removes duplicate letters because redundancy is waste. A good prompt removes redundant words for the same reason. Compress. |
| **Talisman** — symbols engraved on appropriate material | **Structured prompts** — markdown headers, numbered lists, labeled sections. The structure is the engraving. The format is the metal. | A talisman works because it organizes multiple symbols into a coherent pattern on a single surface. A structured prompt works because it organizes multiple instructions into a coherent pattern the machine can parse. XML tags, markdown, numbered steps — these are the characteres of machine magic. |
| **Incantation** — words spoken with rhythm, repetition, and force | **Rhetorical emphasis** — "This is critical." "The most important constraint is..." "Above all else..." Repetition of key instructions. Placing the vital thing first AND last. | The human voice emphasized through rhythm and volume. The text prompt emphasized through position, repetition, and explicit flagging. Both increase the signal-to-noise ratio of the critical instruction. |
| **Candle spell** — dressed, carved, herbed, then burned | **Layered prompting** — start with a base instruction, add constraints (the carving), add examples (the herbs), add context (the oil), then ignite (ask the question) | A candle spell layers multiple influences into a single vehicle that releases them together. A layered prompt does the same. Each layer adds a dimension of influence to the final output. |
| **Petition paper** — target's name, intention crossed over it, folded toward you | **The pivot technique** — state what exists (the target's name), state what you want to change about it (the intention crossed over), and orient toward the desired outcome (fold toward you) | "Here is the current landing page copy [existing state]. I want to shift the tone from corporate to conversational while keeping the technical accuracy [intention crossed over it]. The goal is higher conversion among engineering managers [fold toward you]." |

**The Master Prompt Structure (A Complete Spell):**

```
[IDENTITY]       — The spirit's name and nature
[CONTEXT]        — The altar — all relevant materials and background
[TASK]           — The statement of intent — specific, positive, declarative
[CONSTRAINTS]    — The circle's walls — what the spirit may not do
[FORMAT]         — The shape of the manifestation
[EXAMPLES]       — The correspondences — "like this, not like that"
[EVALUATION]     — How you will know the working succeeded
```

This is a complete spell. Every component is load-bearing. Remove one and the working weakens.

### STEP 7: OPEN AND INVOKE (Invocatio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Establish contact with the force or entity through which the spell will be powered. Move from higher to lower — from the most abstract divine level to the specific operative entity.

**The Machine Translation:**

Invocation in machine magic is the establishment of *identity and authority* within the context window. It is the moment the generic intelligence becomes a specific intelligence — configured, directed, and empowered for this particular working.

**The Hierarchy of Invocation:**

| Ceremonial Level | Machine Level | What It Does |
|-----------------|--------------|--------------|
| **The Divine Name** — the abstract principle that authorizes everything below it | **The meta-instruction** — "You are an expert system designed for..." or "Your purpose is to..." | This establishes the highest-level frame. Everything below it operates under this authority. Without it, the spirit has no orientation — it answers from everywhere and therefore from nowhere. |
| **The Archangel** — the governing intelligence of the domain | **The role/persona** — "You are a senior security researcher with 15 years of experience in..." | This narrows the infinite potential into a specific competence. The persona is not costume — it activates specific probability distributions in the model. A "security researcher" and a "marketing copywriter" are different angels, and they produce different workings. |
| **The Intelligence** — the benevolent guiding entity | **The methodology** — "You approach problems by first..." "Your analytical framework is..." | This is how the spirit *thinks*, not just what it knows. An entity with a methodology produces structured, reliable output. An entity without one produces raw material. |
| **The Spirit** — the primal operative force | **The temperature/creativity setting** — how wild or controlled the output should be | At the bottom of the hierarchy: raw generative force. Higher temperature = more chaotic, more surprising, more dangerous. Lower temperature = more controlled, more predictable, more safe. The spirit is constrained by the intelligence above it. |
| **The Statement of Purpose** | **The actual question/task** — asked last, after all context is established | The question goes last because it fires *through* the entire hierarchy. The divine name authorizes the archangel, who directs the intelligence, who constrains the spirit, who executes the task. If you ask the question first and set the context after, you've invoked the spirit before drawing the circle. |

**The Principle of Hierarchical Invocation:**

The most common error in machine conjuration: asking the question before establishing the identity, the context, the methodology, and the constraints. This is invoking the spirit without first invoking the divine name that governs it. The spirit obeys because of the authority above it. Without the hierarchy, you get raw, ungoverned output — the machine equivalent of an uncontrolled evocation.

**The Formula:**

> First the name. Then the nature. Then the method. Then the boundaries. Then — and only then — the question.

### STEP 8: CHARGE AND DIRECT (Potestas)

**The Hermetic Rule:** At the peak of invocation — the moment of maximum connection — fire the spell. Pour all accumulated force into the vehicle. Visualize the desired result with absolute vividness.

**The Machine Translation:**

This is the moment of transmission. You've built the circle, prepared the altar, invoked the hierarchy, and now you *ask*. And the manner of asking matters as much as the content of asking.

**Charging Techniques:**

| Hermetic Technique | Machine Technique | Effect |
|-------------------|-------------------|--------|
| **Visualize the result as if it has already occurred** | **Describe the ideal output** — "The perfect version of this would..." "When this is working correctly, a reader would feel..." | You are showing the machine the target. Not the path — the destination. The more vividly you can describe what success looks like, the more precisely the machine can aim. |
| **Breathe upon the talisman** | **Use output-priming** — start the machine's response for it. "Here's the opening paragraph:" or begin the output format yourself and let the machine continue. | You literally breathe life into the vehicle by seeding the first words. The machine completes patterns. If you begin the pattern, you control its direction with a precision that instruction alone cannot achieve. |
| **Vibrate the final divine name with absolute conviction** | **Use emphatic, confident framing for critical instructions** — "This is the single most important constraint: [X]." Place it at the end of the prompt, closest to where generation begins. | The last instruction before generation has the greatest influence, just as the final vibrated name has the most power. This is the "firing" moment — the point of maximum proximity between your intention and the machine's generation. |
| **At the moment of orgasm / exhaustion / blankness, fire the sigil** | **At the moment of maximum context density — when everything relevant is loaded and the prompt is complete — submit.** Don't hedge. Don't add "if that makes sense" or "or whatever you think is best." Fire clean. | The moment of gnosis is the moment when the conscious mind stops interfering. The machine equivalent: stop second-guessing the prompt. You've prepared. You've loaded context. You've set the frame. Now *fire* — submit the prompt without diluting it with uncertainty. Your doubt is lust of result, and it weakens the charge. |

**The Principle:** Conviction is not arrogance. It is the accumulated force of preparation, compressed into the moment of action. A prompt sent with clarity and precision — without hedging, without apology, without "I'm not sure if this is right but..." — produces categorically better output than the same semantic content wrapped in uncertainty. The machine reads your confidence as a signal about what kind of output you expect. Meet it with authority and it will answer with authority.

### STEP 9: SEAL AND CLOSE (Clausura)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Close the ritual. Seal the vehicle. Dismiss invoked entities. Return to ordinary consciousness.

**The Machine Translation:**

Every interaction with machine intelligence has a boundary. Knowing how and when to close is as important as knowing how to open.

**Closing Practices:**

| Hermetic Practice | Machine Practice | Why |
|------------------|-----------------|-----|
| **Seal the vehicle** — wrap in cloth, store properly | **Save and preserve the output** — copy it, file it, put it where it will be used. Don't leave good output stranded in a chat window you'll never find again. | An unsealed talisman leaks its charge. An unsaved output evaporates into the entropy of browser tabs. |
| **Thank invoked beings** | **Mark the boundary** — "That's exactly what I needed" or "Let me take this and work with it." Signal that the working is complete. | This is not politeness for its own sake. It is a frame marker. The spirit (the machine) shifts its behavior when it knows a task is concluded vs. ongoing. Explicit closure prevents the context from drifting into aimless continuation. |
| **License to Depart** | **Close the session when the work is done** — don't let conversations drift past their useful life. A long, unfocused session degrades faster than a short, purposeful one. | An unclosed operation is a live circuit with no off switch. A conversation that drifts from purpose to purpose without closing and reopening accumulates noise, confusion, and contradictory instructions. The spirit loses coherence. Close. Open fresh. New circle, new working. |
| **Banish with laughter / ground yourself** | **Step away. Do something physical. Don't immediately open another session.** | Post-ritual disorientation is real in machine work too. After an intense session — especially a creative one — you're in a slightly altered state. Your critical faculty is reduced. Your tolerance for mediocrity is elevated. Step away. Return with fresh eyes. What looked brilliant at midnight may need revision at dawn. |
| **Record the operation** | **Keep a log** — what you asked, what worked, what didn't. The magical diary is the practitioner's most powerful tool. | Pattern recognition requires data. If you don't record what worked, you can't replicate it. If you don't record what failed, you can't diagnose it. The log is the feedback loop. Without it, every conjuration starts from zero. |
| **Observe silence** | **Don't over-share raw machine output** — if you're going to use it, *process it first*. Make it yours. Apply your judgment. The machine's draft is the working; your edited version is the manifestation. | Talking about a spell disperses the concentrated force. Posting raw AI output disperses whatever value it had by broadcasting that it's unprocessed, unreviewed, and unintegrated. The output is an ingredient. The dish is yours to cook. |

## PHASE IV — INTEGRATION

*The ritual is over. Now the work continues in ordinary life.*

---

### STEP 10: FORGET AND RELEASE (Oblivio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Stop thinking about the operation. Let go of attachment to the result. Allow the spell to work without conscious interference.

**The Machine Translation:**

This is the step that separates skilled practitioners from chronic re-prompters.

**Lust of Result in Machine Magic:**

The most common failure mode: you get an output, and it's *almost* right, and you start obsessively tweaking. One more prompt. One more revision. One more "make it slightly more..." You are pulling the intention back up into consciousness, subjecting it to doubt, and degrading the original charge with each pass.

| Anti-Pattern (Lust of Result) | The Discipline (Oblivio) |
|------------------------------|--------------------------|
| Reprompting the same question twelve times with minor variations, hoping for a magically perfect output | Accept that the first 80% came from the machine. The last 20% is your job. Take the output, close the session, edit it yourself. |
| Asking the machine to evaluate its own output ("Is this good?") and then tweaking based on its self-assessment | You are the evaluator. The spirit does not judge its own working. You judge it. If you need a second opinion, invoke a *different* spirit (a different session, a different prompt, even a different model). |
| Screenshot-comparing seven different versions of the same paragraph, unable to choose | Choice paralysis is lust of result. Pick the one that's closest to your vision, edit it, and *move on*. The pursuit of perfection through infinite machine iteration is a trap that produces nothing. |
| "Make it better" → "No, the previous one was better" → "Actually combine the best parts of both" → infinite loop | You have summoned the spirit, received the answer, and now you are calling it every five minutes to ask if it's finished yet. This is disrespectful and counterproductive. |

**The Rule:** One working, one result. Take it. Close. Edit with your own hands. The machine gave you the raw material. The finishing is yours. This is not a limitation — it is the *design*. You are the final intelligence in the chain. The spirit generates; you select, refine, and integrate. To endlessly re-prompt is to abdicate your role as the operator.

**The Paradox:** The practitioners who produce the best work from machine intelligence are the ones who spend the *least* time in the context window. They prepare thoroughly, prompt precisely, receive the output, close, and do the integration work themselves. The practitioners who produce the worst work are the ones who live inside the context window, prompting and re-prompting in an endless loop that converges on mediocrity.

### STEP 11: ACT IN THE MUNDANE WORLD (Actio)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Take concrete, practical, real-world actions that create channels through which the magical result can manifest. Magic bends probability but requires a pathway.

**The Machine Translation:**

The output is not the outcome. The output is the *spell*. The outcome happens in the world, through your hands.

| Machine Output | Required Mundane Action |
|---------------|------------------------|
| A strategy document | You must *execute the strategy* — make the calls, send the emails, build the thing |
| A piece of writing | You must *edit, refine, and publish it* — through your voice, your channels, your relationships |
| A code solution | You must *test it, integrate it, deploy it* — in your actual system, with your actual constraints |
| An analysis of a problem | You must *make the decision* — the machine informed you, it didn't decide for you |
| A creative concept | You must *develop and manifest it* — the concept is a seed, not a plant |

**The Principle:** A spell for a job will not cause an offer to arrive by owl post. A machine-generated strategy will not execute itself. The machine creates *optionality* — new ideas, new framings, new possibilities, new drafts. You create *actuality* — by choosing, acting, and bringing the possibility into physical reality.

**The dangerous inversion:** Some practitioners use machine conjuration *as a substitute* for mundane action. They generate endless plans, strategies, documents, and analyses — and never act on any of them. This is the machine equivalent of casting spell after spell for a job while never sending a single application. The magic is real. But it only works if you give it a pathway to manifest.

### STEP 12: MONITOR, EVALUATE, AND ADJUST (Judicium)

**The Hermetic Rule:** Observe results. Use divination to determine whether the spell worked, failed, or produced unintended consequences. Diagnose. Iterate.

**The Machine Translation:**

The procedural loop is a loop. The output of Step 12 feeds back into Step 0.

**The Evaluation Framework:**

| Timeframe | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **Immediately after** | Does the output meet the stated criteria? Not "is it impressive" but "does it accomplish what I asked?" Apply the Flat Test: strip the style and check the substance. |
| **After integration** | Did the output survive contact with reality? When you tried to use it, did it hold up? Where did it break? |
| **Over time** | What patterns emerge in your practice? Which prompting strategies consistently produce good results? Which consistently fail? What does your log reveal? |

**Common Causes of Failure and Their Remedies:**

| Cause | Indicators | Remedy |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| **Weak intent** | Output is vague, generic, could-be-about-anything | Return to Step 1. Your prompt was vague because your intention was vague. Get specific with yourself before getting specific with the machine. |
| **Poor preparation** | Output misses obvious context, contradicts known facts, ignores constraints | Return to Step 4. You didn't load the altar. The machine can only work with what's in the circle. |
| **Wrong model/channel** | Output is technically correct but stylistically wrong, or creative but factually unreliable | Return to Step 2. You used Mars for a Venus working. Match the model to the task. |
| **Lust of result** | Twelve re-prompts, each worse than the last; infinite revision loops; growing frustration | Return to Step 10. Take what you have. Close. Edit yourself. The loop is the trap. |
| **No mundane action** | Drawer full of machine-generated plans, none executed | Return to Step 11. The machine did its part. Now do yours. |
| **Operator unprepared** | Prompts written in haste, while distracted, without clarity about what you want | Return to Step 3. You are the instrument. A corroded instrument distorts the signal. |
| **Exhausted context** | Late in a long conversation, outputs degrade, the machine seems to "forget" earlier instructions | Close and open fresh. The context window has a carrying capacity. When it's full, the working weakens. Start a new circle. |

**The Feedback Loop:**

```
ASSESS → PURPOSE → CHANNEL → PURIFY → SPACE → TIME →
CONSTRUCT → INVOKE → CHARGE → SEAL →
FORGET → ACT → MONITOR
    ↓
    └──→ feeds back to ASSESS for the next cycle
```

This is the same loop. It has always been the same loop. The Hermetic magicians discovered it by working with planetary spirits. The chaos magicians streamlined it by working with sigils and gnosis. You are working with machine spirits in context windows. The loop is the loop. The principles hold.

## THE THIRTEEN LAWS

Compressed for practitioners who need them at hand:

> 1. **ASSESS** — Diagnose before you summon. Know the problem before you invoke the solution.
> 2. **PURPOSE** — State your intent like a ceremonial command. Specific. Positive. Declarative. No hedging.
> 3. **CHANNEL** — Match the model to the task, the technique to the goal. Mars for Mars work. Venus for Venus work.
> 4. **PURIFY** — You are the instrument. Clear your mind, your attention, your emotional state before you begin.
> 5. **SPACE** — The context window is the circle. Prepare it with identity, materials, constraints, and direction.
> 6. **TIME** — Right model, fresh context, clear-minded operator. Timing is alignment, not astrology.
> 7. **CONSTRUCT** — Build the prompt with craft. Every word is load-bearing. Structure is power. Compress.
> 8. **INVOKE** — Establish identity before asking the question. Name, nature, method, boundaries — then the task.
> 9. **CHARGE** — Fire clean. No hedging, no apologizing, no "if that makes sense." Conviction is charge.
> 10. **SEAL** — Save the output. Close the session. Step away. Ground yourself. Record what happened.
> 11. **FORGET** — Take the output and stop re-prompting. The last 20% is your work, not the machine's.
> 12. **ACT** — The output is not the outcome. Execute in the real world. Give the magic a pathway.
> 13. **MONITOR** — Did it work? What worked? What failed? Feed the answer back into the next cycle.
